EMILY AND DAVID

Saturday, March 11, 2017

About Us

His Story:

September 22, 2010 is a date I will never forget. That was the day I met my future wife. We had been exchanging e-mails for a few weeks (we met on match.com) and decided it was time to meet in person. The decision had been made to meet at Antrim Park to take a short bike ride down the Olentangy Green Ways Trail. I was a little nervous. First impressions are important and I wanted to make it good. I arrived at the agreed upon location at the agreed upon time. I was early so I waited…and waited…and waited. After about 30 minutes I decided that she wasn’t going to show. I packed up my bike and went home. The first thing I did when I arrived home was e-mail her and ask her why she didn’t come to the date? She was very apologetic and explained she had been required to seek shelter at work due to a possible impending tornado. That seemed reasonable enough, despite the fact that where I was the weather was perfect! We did not let the opportunity to meet pass us by and decided to go to my favorite Mexican Restaurant: Las Margaritas. I don’t remember what she ordered, or what she was wearing, but I remember thinking Emily could be the one for me.



Her Story:

In September of 2010, I was perusing messages on Match.com. I got an email from a new cute guy and I thought I would check out his profile further. His profile had a quote “You’ll miss 100% of the chances you don’t take.,” which struck me as such a humble and endearing perspective to take in such a crazy dating world. I’m not sure if it was through David’s photos or our correspondence I learned of our shared passion for bicycles and being active. Our first date was supposed to be a bike ride through Antrim Park, however I guess looking back on it, I technically stood him up. I was kept late at work in the Tornado Shelter because a tornado indeed had touched down in Columbus that afternoon. I remember feeling terrible that I was not going to make it but I was pretty sure he wasn’t going to be crazy enough to go either. I didn’t have his phone number so I sent a message through “Match” to explain that I’m not scared of riding in less than perfect weather, but that I usually draw the line at tornados. I apologized and then left my phone number. I held my breath that he would call and he did! He called later that evening and told me he was there looking for me! We were both hungry so we met at a Mexican restaurant instead. I remember how understanding, genuine and kind he was that evening, qualities that I still love about David today. We did go on a bike ride for our second date and for many dates to follow.
